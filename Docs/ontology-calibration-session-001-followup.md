# Ontology Calibration Session 001 Follow-Up

## Purpose

Reprocess the awkward or bundled items from calibration session 001 using the two new early gates:

```text
CQ-016: product vs personal ontology boundary
CQ-017: claim splitting before axiom review
```

Principle:

```text
Do not review a bundled entry as if it were one claim.
```

## Items Reprocessed

### 1. Pricing, Patents, Debugging Perspectives

**Original friction:** This item bundled multiple business/product claims and felt awkward as one axiom.

**CQ-016 boundary:** `product_system`

**CQ-017 split:** `multiple_claims`

**Split claims:**

1. Pricing should focus on value created.
2. Patent the Understood process before outside testers.
3. Debugging with AI agents is coding and needs its own workflow.

**Result:** Less forced after splitting. Each claim has a different review path. The pricing claim is product strategy, the patent claim is a project/action note, and the debugging claim may become a product/workflow axiom.

## Findings

### What improved

- CQ-016 prevented product/system notes from becoming personal axioms by default.
- CQ-017 made bundled entries easier to reason about.
- The review question shifted from "what is the axiom?" to "how many claims are here?"
- Notifications and Scale ME both became clearer after splitting.

### What still feels awkward

- Observations about another person still need a provenance label beyond `entry_extracted`.
- Product ontology vs personal ontology now has a helper, but no separate product review queue yet.
- Split claims need a UI affordance; doing it mentally is possible but still attention-heavy.

### Missing provenance label

- `observed_other`

### Possible future competency question

```text
Does this item duplicate, revise, or extend an existing item or candidate axiom?
```

Do not build this yet. Session 001 follow-up suggests overlap exists, especially around AI/compute beliefs, but the more immediate need was claim splitting.

## Conclusion

CQ-016 and CQ-017 made the awkward items feel less forced. The ontology should keep these gates before candidate axiom creation:

```text
entry
-> product/personal boundary
-> claim splitting
-> evidence match or candidate axiom
```

The next useful product change is not more ontology logic. It is a split-claim review affordance for bundled entries.
