## Candidate Axioms Worth Reviewing First

### Priority Protocol Positivity

If I frame decisions through ranked life and business priorities, then productivity and satisfaction improve.

Why this matters: this sounds central to Adamâ€™s operating system and was the clearest example of the ontology capturing something practical.

### Notifications Re-Presence Decisions Already Made

Why this matters: this could become a core product axiom for Connections and notifications. The notification is not â€śtime to decideâ€ť; it is â€śyou already decided.â€ť

### Impulse-Buy Running Gels

Why this matters: this links body state to spending behavior in a testable way.

### Compute Over Cleverness

If a hand-crafted solution will plateau, then prefer compute-driven systems.

Why this matters: this is already a repeated self-declared principle and likely belongs in the confirmed-review queue after evidence is attached.

### Coding Insecurity Dissolved by AI Breakthrough

If AI agents remove implementation doubt, then business ambition becomes easier to inhabit.

Why this matters: this is a strong identity/ambition pattern, and it ties AI capability to emotional permission and business action.

## What Worked

- The ontology language is strongest for entries about work, belief, ambition, AI use, product design, and self-management.
- Prepopulation worked much better than asking Adam to originate the structure.
- The canvas let Adam use judgment without spending all attention parsing the workflow.
- The distinction between `remain_note`, `attach_evidence`, and `create_candidate` felt useful.
- The ontology surfaced several high-value candidate axioms from entries that were already sitting unused in the app.
- Adam added a useful correction to the Valentineâ€™s Day item: a personal ontology can honestly represent the affective value of deep relationships.

## What Felt Forced

- Entertainment/reference notes can feel forced if converted into axioms.
- Bundled business notes with multiple ideas feel awkward as single calibration items.
- Entries about another person can become awkward if treated as personal ontology without a clearer provenance label.

## Missing Relationship Types

Possible additions to consider later:

- `reminds`
- `protects_attention`
- `reframes`
- `reduces_friction`

These should not be added automatically. They should be tested against more entries first.

## Missing Provenance Labels

Potential missing label:

Reason: the Stephanie work-withdrawal item is useful, but it is not self-declared and not directly about Adam. `entry_extracted` is technically true but not precise enough.

## Missing Competency Questions


Reason: several entries describe Understood.app as a product system rather than Adamâ€™s personal operating system. The current ontology can hold them, but the scope distinction is not crisp enough.

## UI Friction

- The biggest friction is splitting bundled entries into multiple candidate axioms.
- The current calibration workflow works when the first-pass structure is already filled in.
- Starting from a blank review form is too expensive because it forces Adam to spend attention on sequential organization before judgment.

## Next Changes

1. Add a split-entry affordance for bundled entries.
2. Consider a new provenance label: `observed_other`.
3. Consider a product-vs-personal ontology scope or competency question.
4. Use prefilled review queues for future calibration sessions.
5. Promote only the strongest candidate axioms into the review queue; do not flood it with every plausible pattern.
