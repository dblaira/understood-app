# Ontology Connections Calibration 001

## Purpose

Calibrate existing `connection` entries as potential personal axioms.

Connections are different from ordinary entries. Many are already compressed beliefs, principles, pattern interrupts, or process anchors. This makes them likely ontology seed material, but they still need review before becoming governing axioms.

Review frame:

1. Is this already axiom-like?
2. Is it personal, product/system, or both?
3. What candidate axiom text would it produce?
4. What provenance should it carry?
5. Should it become candidate, evidence, confirmed-review, or remain connection only?

## Summary

- Connections reviewed: 18
- Strong confirmed-review candidates: 7
- Candidate axioms: 6
- Evidence-only connections: 2
- Remain connection only: 3
- Main friction: several connections are product/process principles and should not automatically become personal axioms.

## Strongest Confirmed-Review Candidates

These connections are already stated like governing rules and appear central enough to review for confirmation once evidence is attached.

### Delegation is three sentences

Candidate axiom:

```text
If delegation includes what I want, how I think about it, and what done looks like, then handoff quality improves.
```

Why it matters: this directly matches Adam's working pattern and lowers delegation friction.

### Use compute over cleverness

Candidate axiom:

```text
If hand-crafted solutions will plateau, then use compute over cleverness.
```

Why it matters: this is a recurring operating principle for AI, product, and software work.

### Ten minutes exporting judgment compounds

Candidate axiom:

```text
If I export judgment into a system, then that time compounds; if I only do the task, the value disappears.
```

Why it matters: this is the clearest expression of why ontology work is worth doing.

### Articulating something well changes relationship to it

Candidate axiom:

```text
If I articulate something clearly, then my relationship to it changes because writing is thinking.
```

Why it matters: this supports the whole capture/calibration loop.

### Capture first, structure later

Candidate axiom:

```text
If I capture first and structure later, then I preserve information without blocking action.
```

Why it matters: this is a practical guardrail against over-structuring too early.

### You do not outwork the problem

Candidate axiom:

```text
If I find the structural edge and build the simplest system around it, then results can compound without overwork.
```

Why it matters: this aligns with the deterministic ontology philosophy.

### Judgment strong, vocabulary gap

Candidate axiom:

```text
If I struggle to specify what I want, then the problem is vocabulary, not judgment.
```

Why it matters: this directly explains the best human-agent workflow for Adam.

## Product/System Boundary Notes

Some connections are valuable but may belong to product ontology rather than personal ontology:

- `Every product in this suite fights the same enemy: potential that slips through the cracks of an unexamined day.`
- `Does this help capture potential that would otherwise slip away?`
- `My assignment is to create desire.`

These should not automatically become personal axioms. They should either remain connections or enter a future product ontology lane.

## Evidence Opportunities

The following connections should be treated as evidence targets in future calibration:

- `Anticipatory anxiety lies. Feelings aren't facts.`
- `Work on what I can. Figure out the rest later on.`
- `Momentum is the metric that matters.`
- `Energy moves forward, not backward.`

These are strong principles, but they become more trustworthy when linked to lived entries where the principle helped or failed.

## What Worked

- Connections are far more axiom-like than ordinary entries.
- The existing `connection_type` labels are useful: `validated_principle`, `process_anchor`, `pattern_interrupt`, and `identity_anchor` already imply different review expectations.
- Most connections can be reviewed faster than ordinary entries because they are already compressed.

## What Felt Forced

- Some product principles are easy to mistake for personal axioms.
- Short identity anchors can be powerful but need evidence before confirmation.
- Pattern interrupts are often questions, so they may need conversion into if-then form before ontology review.
