# Ontology Fluency Scorecard

Use this scorecard to prove ontology fluency through 50 counted sessions.

Manual: [`Docs/ontology-fluency-manual.md`](ontology-fluency-manual.md)

## Counting Rule

A session counts when all of these are true:

- You accessed the ontology without searching for instructions.
- You analyzed at least one information item.
- You chose the relevant domain or domains without live explanation.
- You chose the ontology move without live explanation.
- You updated or reviewed the ontology state.
- You recorded the outcome.
- You did not ask AI what a term, metric, or action means.

A session does not count if you needed live explanation. Mark it in the training log instead.

## Allowed Delegation

Delegation does not break a counted session when it only handles maintenance:

- Extract candidate axioms from a batch of notes before or after the counted session, provided you still choose the session move yourself.
- Summarize supporting evidence.
- Prepare a review queue.
- Batch update rows after you have already chosen the move.
- Generate a post-session review after the session is complete.

Delegation breaks a counted session when it replaces your judgment:

- Explaining what a term means.
- Explaining why a measurement matters.
- Choosing the status for you.
- Choosing the next ontology move for you.
- Telling you how to access or update the ontology.
